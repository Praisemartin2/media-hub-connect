# Cult-Brand Reel Style (CapCut)

A reusable editing recipe reverse-engineered from a 48s vertical talking-head
reel about brand psychology, recolored to the user's brand palette. Target
editor: **CapCut** (mobile or desktop). The frames in `reference/` show the
edit mechanics; `reference/palette-swatch.png` and
`reference/color-palette-source.jpg` show the colors to match.

## The style in one sentence

A calm talking-head A-roll shot against a clean background, cut fast and
aggressively punctuated by **full-frame text cards**, **big bold headlines**,
**karaoke captions**, and **keyword-triggered b-roll**, all unified by a warm
**editorial cobalt-blue + rust + cream** palette and a **continuous music bed**.

## Locked specs (match these exactly)

- **Aspect / size:** 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920 export (source was 720×1280).
- **Frame rate:** 30 fps.
- **Length:** 35–50s sweet spot (reference = 48.5s).
- **Pacing:** ~18 hard cuts in 48s → **average shot ≈ 2.5–3s**, but b-roll
  inserts are much shorter (0.4–1.5s "punch-in" cutaways on key words).
- **Audio:** one **continuous music bed** runs the whole time (no silent gaps),
  ducked low under the voiceover. Mix target ~ -16 LUFS, peaks near -1.5 dB.

## Brand palette (this replaces the original neon green)

The look is **warm editorial / magazine**, not neon. Sampled from the user's
reference designs:

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary accent** (headlines, blue cards, kicker pill) | Cobalt / royal blue | `#1E49A6` |
| **Secondary accent / caption highlight** | Rust / terracotta | `#DB835B` |
| **Neutral background** (cream cards, lower-thirds) | Warm cream | `#F0EDE8` |
| **Deep accent / shadows** | Espresso brown | `#4C220C` |
| **Text** | Near-black `#111111` / White `#FFFFFF` | |

Use the palette as the through-line everywhere the original used green:
- Full-frame title cards: alternate **cobalt-blue** (cream/white text) and
  **cream** (cobalt or near-black text); use **rust** sparingly for the punchline.
- Kicker pill: cobalt-blue pill with cream text (or cream pill with cobalt text).
- Karaoke caption highlight word: **rust `#DB835B`**.
- Wardrobe / prop / b-roll color matches: lean cream + rust + cobalt; the
  reference imagery uses a rust blazer on a cream/dark editorial set.

## Typography

One bold **grotesque / Helvetica-style** family throughout (Helvetica Now,
Neue Haas Grotesk, or CapCut's "Aa" bold sans equivalent). Tight line spacing,
heavy weight. Optionally pair with a **script font** for a small accent line
(the reference uses a handwritten "Who we are" under the bold headline). Three
text treatments:

| Treatment | Where | Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Kicker pill** | Top, small | ALL CAPS, letter-spaced. Cream text on a cobalt-blue rounded pill (or cobalt text on cream). Category label e.g. `CULT BRAND PSYCHOLOGY`. Stays on during the hook. |
| **Hook headline** | Over A-roll, lower-third or center | Cobalt-blue **or** white bold, 2–3 short lines, subtle shadow. The scroll-stopping promise e.g. `How cult brands create emotion`. |
| **Title card phrase** | Full-frame interstitial | Big bold **italic**, left-aligned, on a solid **cobalt OR cream** screen. Single phrase used as punctuation e.g. `When they feel`, `Don't be a faceless company`. |

Plus **karaoke captions** (see below).

## The four text systems — how each is built in CapCut

### 1. Karaoke captions (auto, word-highlight)
1. `Text → Auto captions` → generate from the voiceover.
2. Style them small, centered, lower-third, white, bold, with a thin
   shadow/stroke so they read on any background.
3. Turn on the **highlight** option (CapCut "Auto captions → highlight word")
   and set the highlight color to the rust **`#DB835B`**. The active/keyword
   gets the rust pop (see `reference/karaoke-caption-broll.png` for placement).

### 2. Kicker pill + hook headline (the first 2 seconds)
- Add a text box, type the category in caps, give it a cobalt-blue background
  pill (CapCut: text `Bubble`/background option, rounded, `#1E49A6`).
- Add a second large headline below it (cobalt or white). Keep it on screen
  through the hook.
- Animation: subtle `Pop`/`Rise` in, no exit until the first cut.

### 3. Full-frame title cards (the rhythm punctuation)
- Cut the A-roll, drop a solid color clip (cobalt or cream) for **0.4–1.0s**.
- Add the phrase in big bold italic, left-aligned, in the contrasting color
  (cream/white text on cobalt; cobalt/near-black text on cream).
- Animation: fast `Scale`/`Pop` in. Alternate cobalt ↔ cream between cards;
  reserve a **rust** card for the single biggest punchline.
- Use these on emphatic words ("and", "this", the punchline) to create rhythm.
- A few cards in the reference use a **doubled/offset "echo" glitch** on the
  text — duplicate the text layer, offset it a few px, tint the copy (rust on a
  cobalt card), and add a quick shake/glitch animation for that effect.

### 4. B-roll cutaways (keyword-synced)
- Whenever the VO names a concrete thing, **punch to b-roll for 0.4–1.5s** then
  cut back to A-roll. Examples from the reference:
  - product/brand named → product close-up, application macro
  - "faceless company" → planes with cartoon eyes drawn on (literal visual gag)
  - "loyal to friends" → a relatable sitcom meme clip, letterboxed in a centered
    frame on a cream/dark background
- Keep b-roll consistent with the cream + rust + cobalt palette where possible.

## Build order (do it in this sequence)

1. **Set the project:** 9:16, 30fps.
2. **Lay the A-roll** (the talking-head take) on the main track, trim filler/ums
   so it's tight and fast.
3. **Add the music bed** on an audio track, loop/trim to full length, lower its
   volume and enable **auto-ducking** under the voice. No silent gaps.
4. **Auto-captions** → style → rust highlight word.
5. **Hook (0–2s):** cobalt kicker pill + headline.
6. **Title cards:** insert full-frame cobalt/cream phrase cards at emphasis beats.
7. **B-roll:** add keyword-synced punch-ins; cut on the beat.
8. **Color:** clean, warm editorial grade (CapCut `Adjust`: gentle contrast,
   slightly lifted/warm whites toward cream, natural saturation — do NOT push
   neon). Keep skin natural; let cobalt and rust be the only loud colors.
9. **Transitions:** mostly **hard cuts**. Sparingly use a quick zoom-punch or
   whip between a couple of b-roll hits — do NOT over-transition.
10. **Polish pass:** add subtle whoosh/riser SFX on the biggest title-card hits,
    re-check every cut lands on a speech beat, export 1080×1920.

## Quality bar (check before exporting)

- [ ] Hook headline + kicker visible within the first second.
- [ ] No shot longer than ~3s without a cut, caption change, or b-roll.
- [ ] Palette appears in: kicker pill (cobalt), caption highlight (rust), at
      least one cobalt and one cream title card, and a wardrobe/prop/b-roll element.
- [ ] **No green anywhere** — the accent is cobalt + rust on cream, not neon.
- [ ] Music bed continuous and ducked; voice always intelligible.
- [ ] Every b-roll insert is justified by a word in the VO.
- [ ] Captions are accurate and never cover faces or the headline.
- [ ] Ends on a CTA card (the reference ends on `Comment a brand below`).

## Scripting note (why the edit works)

The reference structures the VO as: **hook → "it's called X" reveal → proof /
examples → contrarian "don't be boring" line → CTA.** The editing amplifies that
arc — title cards land on the reveal and the punchline, b-roll lands on the
examples, and the closing CTA card ends it. When editing new footage, map the
title cards to the script's turning points, not at random.
