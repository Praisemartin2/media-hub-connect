## POST A — "Washington just made its biggest housing push in years" (5 slides)

Washington is making its biggest move on housing affordability in years. Here's what buyers should actually take from it 👇

In March, the White House signed **Executive Order 14393 — "Promoting Access to Mortgage Credit"** — aimed at making mortgage credit cheaper and easier to get, especially through community and smaller banks. A companion order directs federal agencies to cut the red tape that slows and inflates new home construction.

The bigger lever: the administration's plan directs **Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac to buy up to $200 billion in mortgage-backed securities** to push rates down — and officials say they're "actively evaluating" **portable mortgages** you could carry from one home to the next.

The reality check ⚖️ As of mid-June 2026, the 30-year fixed is still hovering around **6.5%**, and the Fed is widely expected to **hold rates** at this week's meeting. The sub-6% level the plan is chasing hasn't been seen since 2022.

Bottom line: if lower rates + more supply actually arrive, the buyers who get pre-positioned now will be first in line. 🏡

## POST B — "Atlanta is rewriting its zoning code — first time in 40 years" (5 slides)

Atlanta is about to change how the whole city gets built — and most buyers haven't heard about it yet 👇

**"ATL Zoning 2.0"** would replace Atlanta's roughly **40-year-old zoning code** with a simpler, more predictable framework. **City Council** is expected to take it up in **spring/early summer 2026**, and it could reshape what gets built — and where — for decades.

Why it matters: it's the backbone of Atlanta's push for **more homes, and more types of homes.** Mayor Dickens pledged **20,000 affordable units by 2030** and the city is already **past halfway (12,441)**, while the **BeltLine** has hit **79%** of its affordable-housing goal (4,425 units). Still, metro Atlanta **lost 250,000 homes priced under $1,250/mo** between 2019–2024.

The market backdrop 📊 Median sale price is steady around **$435K**, homes are taking about **55 days** to sell (down ~14% YoY), and inventory is rising — a more **balanced market** that hands buyers real negotiating room.

Bottom line: more supply + more leverage = a window for prepared buyers. Let's get you positioned. 🏡
