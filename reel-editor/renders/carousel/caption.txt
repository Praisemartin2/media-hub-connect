Red flags to look for when touring a home 🚩

Agents catch these in the first 5 minutes of a showing. Now you will too 👇

Before you fall in love with the kitchen, walk through this checklist:

1️⃣ Fresh paint in just one spot — a single freshly painted wall or ceiling patch is rarely cosmetic. It's often hiding a water stain, smoke damage, or mold. Ask what's underneath.

2️⃣ Doors that stick or won't latch — doors and windows that jam or leave big uneven gaps can point to foundation movement, not just "an old house." Open and close a few on every floor.

3️⃣ A wall of air fresheners — plug-ins, candles, and fresh cookies in every room can be a cover for moisture, pets, or mildew. Trust your nose, not the vanilla.

4️⃣ Sloping or bouncy floors — if floors tilt, dip, or feel spongy, that's one of the clearest structural tells. Set a marble down… if it rolls on its own, ask questions.

5️⃣ Stained ceilings & warped trim — brown rings, bubbling paint, or swollen baseboards mean leaks, past or active. Always ask for the repair history.

The home you buy should be a flex, not a fixer you didn't sign up for. 🏡
